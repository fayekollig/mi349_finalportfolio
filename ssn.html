Spartan Support Network

Director of Marketing

Creating and implementing a brand for a student organization

Background

Spartan Support Network is a student-led organization on MSU campus dedicated to providing safe spaces for students to talk openly and form genuine connections. It was founded in 2017 as a branch of the non-profit The Support Network, that facilitates and supports branches in (as of Fall 2020) eight schools across the country. The organization hosts weekly groups that take place at several times on several nights throughout the week that any MSU student can join. The weekly groups are led by group leaders, and the group leaders are supported and led by a board of directors. I was involved with SSN for two years, first as a member and then a group leader, before becoming the director of marketing. The scope of this position involved managing apparel, outreach, and social media. Prior to the summer of 2020, SSN had not had a cohesive visual brand that was developed consistently across its internet presence.

Process

As a part of my original interview process, I developed a marketing strategy to improve engagement and following on our Instagram and Facebook platforms. Beginning in May of 2020, I developed a unique visual brand for SSN, using a specific font package, color palette, and logo to unify the appearance of our posts. I sought direction from comparable social media presences of organization like Mental Health America, other MSU organizations, and other branches of The Support Network, along with conversations with the rest of the SSN directors, to develop a content strategy for SSN that fit with our mission statement and the role we wanted to play on campus. Throughout the summer of 2020 and the Fall 2020 Semester, I designed and implemented several Instagram and Facebook campaigns that aligned with our values as an organization, including content for Mental Health Awareness Month, Black Mental Health Awareness Month, BIPOC Mental Health Month, Leader Spotlight Saturdays, Monday Positivity, and a series of “Let’s Talk About it” posts focusing on student mental wellness topics. Outside of posts, I also sought to build our presence on campus as a mental wellness resource, and throughout the fall semester we have worked with or interfaced with organizations like the University Activities Board, ASMSU, Love Yourself Clothing, Impact Radio, OCAT, and Broad Student Orgs.

Individual Skills and Responsibilities

As the Director of Marketing, I:

  • Used Adobe Spark, Photoshop, Instagram, and Facebook to create and post content
  • Handled social media outreach to post and repost related organizations
  • Researched and developed resource guides on mental wellness topics
  • Worked with a third-party organization to develop apparel from design to sale
  • Communicated progress and key decisions in weekly meetings with a director board
